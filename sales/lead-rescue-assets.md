# Sales Assets — Lead Rescue for Trade Contractors

## 1. Warm-intro ask (text Dad can forward)

> Hey [Name] — my son runs a service that answers contractors' missed calls and calls the customer
> back within a minute so the job doesn't go to the next guy on Google. He's taking on 3 local
> [trade] companies at a discount to prove it out. Worth a 10-minute call? His number: [X].

Keep it in Dad's voice. No "AI" anywhere.

## 2. Cold email (for non-network outreach, after first 2 wins)

**Subject:** You missed my call yesterday

> Hi [Name] — I called [Company] Tuesday at 4:40pm about a [water heater / AC quote] and got
> voicemail. So did everyone else who called after 4 that day, and most of them called the next
> company on the list.
>
> We answer those calls and call every web lead back within 60 seconds, qualify them, and book them
> on your calendar. [Local Co.] booked $14k in jobs their first month from calls they'd have missed.
>
> Want me to run a free missed-call audit on your line this week? Takes nothing on your end —
> I'll send you one page showing exactly what's slipping through.

## 3. Cold DM (short form)

> Called your office twice this week — voicemail both times. I run missed-call rescue for [trade]
> companies in the Bay Area: we answer, call leads back in 60 seconds, and book them on your
> calendar. Free audit shows what it's costing you. Interested?

## 4. Discovery call questions

1. Who answers the phone when you're on a job? What happens after hours?
2. How many calls/leads a week, roughly? Where do they come from (Google, LSA, Angi, referral)?
3. What's an average ticket? A big one?
4. When someone requests a quote and goes quiet — who follows up? When?
5. How many open quotes are sitting out there right now?
6. What do you spend on leads/ads per month?
7. If your calendar had 3 extra booked jobs next month, what would that be worth?

(Q3 × Q1 gives us the lost-revenue math for the close. Q5–6 sets up the quote-chasing upsell.)

## 5. Pitch / demo script (10 min)

1. **Their numbers back at them:** "You get ~40 calls a week, average ticket $2,800, and nights and
   weekends go to voicemail. If just 2 a week are slipping, that's ~$5–20k/month."
2. **The audit result** (if run): show the log of our test calls.
3. **What we do:** "Missed call → your customer gets a text in 10 seconds → we call them back
   within a minute, qualify, and book them on your calendar. Every open quote gets followed up on
   day 2, 5, and 10 until it's a yes or a no. You get a one-page report every Friday: calls
   rescued, jobs booked, revenue recovered."
4. **Live demo:** call the demo Twilio line from their cell, let it ring out, watch the text-back
   arrive on their phone in real time. (This demo is 20 minutes of setup and closes deals.)
5. **Offer:** founding-client terms below. "If we don't book you [3] jobs in 30 days from calls
   you'd have missed, you don't pay."

## 7. Objection handling

- **"I already have an answering service."** "They take messages. Messages aren't bookings. We call
  the lead back in 60 seconds, qualify them, and put them on your calendar — and we chase your open
  quotes. Did your answering service book you a job last week?"
- **"My wife/office manager handles calls."** "Great — we're not replacing her, we're catching
  what happens at 7pm, at lunch, and when she's on the other line. The audit will show whether
  anything's slipping. If nothing is, I'll tell you and leave you alone."
- **"AI answering? My customers hate robots."** "For the first months it's not a robot — it's us,
  on the phone, with your scripts. Customers just experience a company that picks up."
- **"Too expensive."** "One recovered [water heater / roof repair] pays for four months. And the
  first month is guaranteed: no booked jobs, no bill."
- **"I don't want to change my phone system."** "Nothing changes. One forwarding setting — calls
  you answer today, you still answer. We only exist when you *don't* pick up."

## 8. Onboarding checklist (founding clients)

1. 30-min kickoff: services offered, service area, pricing ranges, calendar access, emergency rules.
2. Set conditional call-forwarding (no-answer / busy → Twilio number). Test together on the spot.
3. Write their qualification script (5 questions, their language).
4. Collect open-quote list for chasing (Pro tier).
5. Send day-3 check-in, then Friday report every week.

## 9. Follow-up sequence (for prospects who don't close)

- Day 2: one-line recap + audit offer restated.
- Day 7: case study / result from another client ("$X booked in month one").
- Day 21: "Still missing calls? Audit offer stands." Then quarterly, forever, with fresh numbers.

## 10. Simple agreement terms (1-pager, not legalese)

Month-to-month, cancel anytime, 30-day performance guarantee for founding tier, client owns all
call data and phone numbers, we carry no liability for jobs performed, prices locked 12 months.
